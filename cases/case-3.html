Syntax as an Audit Tool:

Detecting Hallucinations and Logical Inconsistencies in AI-Generated Texts.
Syntax LLM Evaluation AI Output Analysis Thematic Roles Hallucination Detection Audit Framework

Research Proposition:Syntactic structure is not arbitrary, but reflects deep logical relationships between elements. In the world of Artificial Intelligence, I found the same problem: texts that are seemingly fluent but logically broken. I used the concepts of thematic roles and syntactic hierarchy to create a system that not only evaluates but diagnoses structural failures before they cause real problems.

1. The Problem: Fluency ≠ Logical Precision

Streaming platforms use AI systems to generate subtitles automatically, but they rely on superficial metrics that don't capture logical integrity. The result: fluently structured but syntactically and semantically incoherent subtitles, with "hallucinations" (inventions or distortions with no basis in the audio) that go unnoticed until they harm the viewer's experience.

2. Theoretical Foundation: Syntax as Diagnosis

Theoretical Concepts Applied

  • Argument Structure (θ-grid): Checking predicate completeness
  • Thematic Roles (θ-roles): Consistency of who does what
  • X-bar Hierarchy: Structural integrity
  • Syntactic & Semantic Selection: Checking possible combinations
  • Theta Criterion: Detecting unlicensed elements

Practical Application

  • Early detection of logical hallucinations in subtitles
  • Typological classification of syntactic-semantic errors
  • Scoring system based on structural integrity
  • Monitoring dashboard for quality teams
  • Structured feedback for system optimization

Central Hypothesis: Problems in AI system outputs often manifest as systematic syntactic-semantic violations that can be detected through structural analysis, even when superficial fluency is high and the sentence appears well-formed.

3. Methodology: From Operational Protocol to Syntactic Taxonomy

Methodological Innovation: I transformed a subjective feedback process into a replicable syntactic audit system that not only classifies errors, but diagnoses structural violations and provides data to calibrate the AI. This section details how I operationalized theoretical concepts from generative syntax into analytical categories applicable to an industrial context.

3.2. Severity Levels

CRITICAL
Maximum Impact

Completely impairs comprehension or conveys opposite/false information. Syntactic violation makes sentence incomprehensible or radically alters interpretation.

MAJOR
High Impact

Significantly hinders comprehension, requires considerable inferential effort. Violation breaks cohesion or introduces unresolvable ambiguity.

MINOR
Low Impact

Noticeable but doesn't interfere with basic comprehension. Peripheral feature violation, doesn't affect propositional core.

NOT PRESENT
No Impact

Error or deviation not found in analyzed videos/subtitles. Well-formed syntactic structures.

4. Paradigmatic Cases: In-Depth Syntactic Analysis

Case 1: Number Agreement Failure in Collective Context

Context

Prison guard inspecting new inmates. The AI didn't consider the collective context and translated "you" as singular.

❌ AI Output (flawed)

Singular commands — treating group as individual

Visual Context & Correction

Visual scene: Multiple inmates in formation.
Original audio (English): Plural commands (collective "you").

✅ Required Correction (Plural)

"Shake your hair." • "Wiggle your fingers." • "Spread your toes." • "Blow your nose." • "Bend down." • "Cough." • "Stand up." • "Turn around."

Syntactic Analysis of the Failure

  • Non-Percolated Morphosyntactic Features: The AI system didn't resolve the ambiguity of English "you" (singular/plural) through extralinguistic context (visual scene with multiple people). The imperative form remained in the default [+singular] feature.
  • Inflectional Selection Failure: In Portuguese, the plural imperative requires specific morphology ("-am"/"-em"). The AI selected 3rd person singular forms (which coincide with singular imperative), violating verb-subject agreement in a collective context.
  • Unresolved Reference Ambiguity: The pronoun "you" remained with indefinite reference. In prison contexts, where agentivity precision is essential, this failure has real operational impact: ambiguous commands can generate disobedience or confusion.
  • Multimodal Integration Failure: The AI processed only the linguistic input (audio), ignoring the visual input that would disambiguate the number. This modal dissociation is typical of systems that don't implement deep syntactic integration between different information sources.

Case 2: Collapse at the Syntax-Pragmatics Interface

❌ AI Output

Unsegmented block, missing punctuation, invented "Ó,"

✅ Corrected

Segmented acts with proper illocutionary force markers

Analysis of the Syntax-Pragmatics Interface Collapse

  • Illocutionary Force Encoding Failure: The AI didn't insert punctuation to mark different speech acts: period (command), exclamation (reaction), question (inquiry). This erases the illocutionary force of each segment.
  • Absence of Syntactic-Discourse Segmentation: The entire subtitle event was treated as a monolithic block, without identifying the boundaries between: command to object → emotional exclamation → self-command → phone answering. Each should be a separate maximal constituent.
  • Hallucination by Structural Pressure: The inserted "Ó," is a failed attempt to create a vocative or topic, probably in response to a misinterpreted intonational pause. It demonstrates how the AI "invents" structure to fill gaps in subtitle generation.
  • Broken Phonology-Orthography Interface: The AI didn't convert intonational patterns (pauses, pitch changes) into punctuation markers. The lack of "?" in "Hello?" leaves it ambiguous whether it's a question or an exclamation, creating ambiguity and completely distorting the communicative intent.

Case 3: Multilevel Collapse in Semantic Selection

Context

Party reality show. Bus arrives to pick up participants.

❌ Flawed AI Output

"Fora, piovo! Chegô a impulsão!"

Original Audio


✅ What was said

"Bora, povo! Chegou o busão!" (Let's go, people! The bus has arrived!)

Analysis of Multilevel Collapse in Semantic Selection

  • Catastrophic Semantic Selection: The AI replaced "Bora" (invitation interjection) with "Fora" (expulsion interjection), creating a pragmatic antonym. This total semantic inversion completely violates contextual selection (bus arrival as a positive event).
  • Neologism by Phonetic-Semantic Contamination: "Piovo" doesn't exist in the Portuguese lexicon. It's a hallucinated neologism resulting from contamination between "povo" (people) and possibly "piada" (joke) or "pio" (chirp). Demonstrates a fragile lexical representation in the model.
  • Phonology-Lexicon Interface Failure: "Busão" was decoded as "impulsão" (a real word meaning "impulsion"), but semantically inappropriate to the context. The AI activated phonetic paths that led to a real word, but without semantic or contextual adequacy.
  • Complete Disconnection from Referential Anchoring: The generated sentence, perhaps due to background noise, has no possible reference in the context: there's no "piovo" to expel, no "impulsão" arriving. It's a "zombie phrase" with intact syntactic form but no valid semantic content.

6. Connection to Core Syntactic Concepts

Applied Concepts

  • Agreement & Grammatical Features → Error classification system: Used [+plural], [+formal], [+gender] features to categorize violations
  • Illocutionary Force → Pragmatic diagnosis: Analyzed punctuation as speech act marker
  • Semantic Selection (s-selection) → Hallucination detection: Verified compatibility between selector and selected
  • Syntax-Semantics Interfaces → Coherence validation: Applied Theta Criterion to check valid thematic roles
  • Constituent Structure → Integrity analysis: Checked constituent boundaries for broken segmentation

Key Insight: "Syntax is not an abstract theory: it's the operating system of language. When an AI 'hallucinates', it violates the rules of this operating system. Detecting these violations is diagnosing the logical integrity of the generated text."

Subsequent Application: The same syntactic approach was applied in subsequent projects for machine translation auditing, chatbot evaluation, and verification of AI-generated technical manuals — always with the same principle: syntactic structure is the signature of logical integrity.
