Training Data Provenance Through Output Analysis

Investigating patterns and source artifacts in AI-generated localization.
LLM Evaluation AI Output Analysis Training Data Digital Forensics Hallucination Detection Provenance

1. Epistemological Positioning

Research Proposition: This study investigates recurrent patterns in AI-generated subtitles for streaming platforms. It treats output anomalies not as merely technical errors but as traces of the underlying training data composition. The research develops methodological approaches for tracing data provenance through linguistic and cultural artifacts found in commercial AI use.

In industrial-scale automated localization, some generated content patterns dismissed as "hallucinations" reveal traceable connections to training data sources and curation practices. This research addresses a core methodological question in linguistic data science: how can we systematically trace the provenance of training data through the analysis of model outputs?

2. Research Context: Artifact Detection at Scale

The Empirical Challenge

  • Data Source: AI-generated subtitles and localization outputs from major streaming platforms (2021-2023)
  • Scale: Industrial deployment affecting millions of users daily
  • Pattern: Systematic appearance of content with identifiable source characteristics
  • Research Gap: Limited methodologies for artifact data provenance analysis

Methodological Approach

  • Digital Forensics: Tracing text patterns to probable training data origins
  • Artifact Classification: Taxonomies for output-source relationships
  • Provenance Framework: Protocols for detecting connections between model outputs and training data
  • Cross-Disciplinary Analysis: Data science + archival research + linguistics

4. Paradigmatic Cases: Methodological Application

Case A: Volunteer-Sourced Data Traces (Amara.org)

Figure 1: Output artifact showing preserved platform attribution pattern

Methodological Insights

  • Volunteer-Sourced Data Detection: Training commercial AI on volunteer-created content creates identifiable output patterns
  • Attribution as Provenance Evidence: Preserved attribution patterns serve as forensic markers
  • Dataset Sustainability: Inclusion without compensation raises ethical questions

Case B: Cross-Platform Pattern Replication

Figure 2: "Subscribe and hit the bell" — YouTube CTA replicated in inappropriate contexts

Methodological Insights

  • Cross-Domain Pattern Transfer: Platform-specific patterns replicated without functional understanding
  • Training Data Contamination: Evidence of inadequate data curation
  • Platform Signature Analysis: Enables identification of specific training data sources

Case C: Unauthorized Content Inclusion

Figure 3: Professional translation agency content incorporated without authorization

Methodological Insights

  • Unauthorized Content Detection: Professional agency content in training data creates identifiable patterns
  • Recursive Training Analysis: Systems trained on outputs of workers they may displace
  • Dataset Compliance: Gaps in data sourcing protocols revealed

Extended: The Fansubs Ecosystem

The Amara.org case exists within a broader ecosystem of volunteer-created localization data (fansubs), introducing additional complexity:

  • Cascading Provenance: Multiple layers of unattributed source material
  • Labor Obfuscation: Volunteer work detached from original context
  • Attribution Loss: Systematic removal of fan credits in dataset processing
  • Semantic Drift: Loss of context leads to hallucinations

5. Methodological Framework: Analysis as Research Practice

Inclusion as Criterion

All legitimate variants and artifacts treated as valid data points, not "errors" — comprehensive source documentation

Transparency in Classification

Explicit rationale documentation, making decisions visible and contestable

Contextual Analysis

Each artifact analyzed within specific cultural, economic, and platform context

Reproducibility

Step-by-step protocols with evidence chains for replication

7. Relevance for AI Training & Linguistic Data Science

This case demonstrates that AI output anomalies are systematic and analyzable — they reveal training data composition and curation practices. Methodological contributions apply directly to:

  • LLM Evaluation: Structured frameworks for output analysis
  • Training Data Curation: Detection of contamination and unauthorized content
  • Linguistic QA: Beyond surface errors to provenance and ethics
